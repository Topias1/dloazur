<behavior>
<!-- Test expectations FIRST (TDD). Assert against the expert-corrected baseline, not raw mockup. -->
DoseEngine::compute(array $mesures, float $volume): array returns an ordered array of recommendation cards. Each card: param, current, target, product, dose, note (ending in a re-test reminder), plus a safety block reference. Ordering follows the treatment sequence TAC then pH then (TH) then chlore then stabilisant then sel (audit section 7).

Test cases (exact, from 05-VALIDATION.md + 05-DIAGNOSTIC-EXPERT-AUDIT.md section 9):
- pH low: volume=50, pH=7.0 yields pH+ (carbonate de soude) ~300 g (steps * 10 g/m3 * volume); target 7.2-7.4; note ends with re-test.
- pH high: volume=50, pH=7.8 yields a SINGLE capped pH- formula (audit P1: drop 3.3, keep only 3.4), capped to ~0.2 pH per application, governed by TAC; never a final one-shot dose.
- TAC low: volume=50, TAC=60 yields TAC+ bicarbonate de sodium (~18 g/m3 per 10 ppm — unchanged, exact).
- Stabilisant high over 75 yields a partial-drain fraction calc; Stabilisant low under 20-30 yields a NEW trigger: propose stabilisant apport (audit P1 tropical root-cause).
- Sel low under 3000 (only if sel=true) yields a kg-sel delta calc toward ~4000 (worded "valeur preconisee par le fabricant, a defaut ~4000").
- Chlore bas under 1 yields RATTRAPAGE ~3-4 g/m3 (NOT 15) — expert P0; a SEPARATE chloreChoc() stays at 15 (leger) / 30 (algues).
- Chlore haut over 3 yields aucun ajout, laisser baisser.
- odeur-forte break-point: if chlore_total given then combine = total - libre then dose 10x; else generous shock + aeration + re-test.
- eau dure: if TH over 250-300 then shock switches to hypochlorite de sodium (not calcium).
- Ordering + gate: a mixed measurement set returns cards ordered TAC then pH then chlore then stab then sel; the chloration card is blocked/withheld while pH or TAC are out of range.
- No false precision: no dose string carries 3 significant figures; rounding is cautious (down on acid/chlore).
</behavior>
